TAX PLANNING

Income Tax Planning Strategies
for Businesses in FY 2025-26

Expert strategies to optimise your business tax liability while maintaining full compliance with Income Tax Act provisions and latest amendments.

CA Priya Mehta January 28, 2026 9 min read
Back to Knowledge Bank
TAX PLANNING CA Priya Mehta January 28, 2026 9 min read

With FY 2025-26 well underway, businesses have a limited window to implement tax planning strategies before the financial year closes. Proactive tax planning is not about evasion — it is about intelligently leveraging legitimate provisions of the Income Tax Act to minimise your tax outgo while staying fully compliant. This article outlines the most impactful strategies for businesses in the current fiscal year.

"Tax planning done in April saves lakhs. Tax planning done in March saves thousands. Start now — every month lost is money left on the table."

1. Choosing the Right Tax Regime

For FY 2025-26, businesses and professionals operating as individuals/HUFs must carefully evaluate the New Tax Regime vs. Old Tax Regime:

New Tax Regime — Slab Rates (FY 2025-26)

Income Slab Tax Rate
Up to ₹3,00,000 Nil
₹3,00,001 – ₹7,00,000 5%
₹7,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 10%
₹10,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 15%
₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000 20%
Above ₹15,00,000 30%

Businesses with significant deductions (HRA, 80C investments, home loan interest, donations, etc.) may still benefit from the old regime. A break-even analysis must be done at the beginning of the year — not at filing time.

2. Key Tax Planning Strategies for FY 2025-26

01

Maximise Depreciation on Business Assets

If your business acquired new plant & machinery or equipment during FY 2025-26, claim additional depreciation of 20% (40% for manufacturing units in notified backward areas) under Section 32(1)(iia). Ensure assets are put to use before 31 March 2026 to claim full-year depreciation on them.

02

Section 80JJAA — Deduction for New Employees

Businesses that added new employees during the year can claim a 30% deduction on additional employee costs under Section 80JJAA for 3 consecutive years. Applicable where the total employment increases and each new employee earns less than ₹25,000/month. Ensure EPF records and employment documentation are in order.

03

R&D Expenditure Deduction (Section 35)

Businesses investing in in-house research and development can claim 100% deduction on revenue and capital expenditure on approved R&D under Section 35(1)(i) and 35(1)(iv). Pharmaceutical, tech and manufacturing companies should evaluate this — it provides a straight deduction over and above the actual expense.

04

Presumptive Taxation — Section 44AD & 44ADA

Small businesses with turnover up to ₹3 crore (₹75 lakh for professionals under 44ADA) can opt for presumptive taxation and declare 8% (6% for digital receipts) or 50% of gross receipts as profit, without maintaining detailed books. This significantly reduces compliance burden and in many cases, lowers effective tax.

05

Set Off and Carry Forward of Losses

If your business incurred losses in prior years, ensure you are correctly carrying forward and setting off those losses against current year profits. Business losses can be carried forward for 8 years and set off against business income. Speculative business losses and capital losses have different carry-forward rules — ensure proper segregation.

06

Donation to Approved Charities (Section 80G)

Donations to PMNRF, CMs Relief Fund, and other approved entities qualify for 100% deduction under Section 80G with no ceiling. Other approved charitable organisations qualify for 50% deduction, subject to 10% of adjusted gross total income cap. Ensure the donee is registered under 80G and you obtain Form 10BE from them.

07

Structuring Remuneration to Partners / Directors

In partnerships and LLPs, salary and interest to working partners (subject to Section 40(b) limits) is deductible at the firm level. Structuring remuneration optimally can shift income from a 30% entity-level tax to lower individual slab rates for partners.

08

Advance Tax — Avoid Interest Under 234B & 234C

Businesses with tax liability exceeding ₹10,000 must pay advance tax in instalments. Missing instalments attracts interest @ 1% per month under sections 234B and 234C. For FY 2025-26, the final advance tax instalment (100%) was due on 15 March 2026. If missed, pay immediately to minimise interest accumulation.

3. Advance Tax Instalment Schedule (FY 2025-26)

Instalment Due Date Minimum % of Liability to Pay
1st Instalment 15 June 2025 15%
2nd Instalment 15 September 2025 45%
3rd Instalment 15 December 2025 75%
4th Instalment 15 March 2026 100%

4. Pre-Year-End Checklist for Businesses

  • Review all deductions available under Chapter VIA and ensure investments/payments are completed before 31 March 2026.
  • Ensure all expenses are booked in the current year for which accrual has occurred.
  • Review outstanding debtors — write off genuinely irrecoverable bad debts before year-end (Section 36(1)(vii)).
  • Evaluate whether bonus/commission to employees should be paid before 31 March to claim deduction in FY 2025-26.
  • Confirm TDS deductions are correctly made on all payments — contractors, rent, professional fees, salary — and deposited timely.
  • Review whether any capital assets can be sold to offset capital gains against available capital losses.
  • Ensure all 80G donations are backed by Form 10BE from the donee institution.

Important: Tax planning must always be supported by genuine commercial transactions and proper documentation. Arrangements entered into purely for tax avoidance without economic substance are subject to the General Anti-Avoidance Rule (GAAR) under Chapter X-A of the Income Tax Act.

5. Corporate Tax — Key Rates for FY 2025-26

Entity Type Base Tax Rate Surcharge (if applicable) Effective Rate (approx.)
Domestic Company (Section 115BAB — new manufacturing) 15% 10% 17.01%
Domestic Company (Section 115BAA — concessional) 22% 10% 25.17%
Domestic Company (TO ≤ ₹400 Cr) 25% 7% / 12% 26–29.12%
Domestic Company (TO > ₹400 Cr) 30% 12% 34.944%
LLP / Firm 30% 12% 34.944%

Companies that have not yet opted for Section 115BAA should evaluate this one-time switch — it may offer significant savings, especially where the MAT credit position is not significant.
